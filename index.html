A Century of Stories

The Lodge & The Diner

A Santa Cruz Mountains landmark since 1900 — and we're proud to feed it.

In 1900, Judge James Harvey Logan bought the old Grover Lumber Mill on the San Lorenzo River and turned it into a creekside resort.

By 1922, Dr. F.K. Camp had hired architect Horace Cotton to do something nobody had done — build a dining room over the creek itself. The result was the legendary Brook Room: a restaurant with a real running brook flowing through it. Trout swam under the tables.

"I went to the Brookdale Lodge / where they have the brook indoors…" — from one of three swing-era hits the Lodge inspired.

For decades this was the second-most-popular resort in California. Pres. Herbert Hoover stayed here. So did Marilyn Monroe, Mae West, Joan Crawford, Tyrone Power, Rita Hayworth, and Hedy Lamarr. The biggest big bands in the country played the ballroom; UN diplomats slipped up from San Francisco in '45.

Down the road lived a tiny Shirley Temple and Tarzan himself, Johnny Weissmuller.

And there's Sarah — the little girl ghost still said to wander the halls. Ghost Adventures filmed here in 2012. Locals will tell you they've heard her.

Today the Lodge is back, and the diner downstairs is run by a Santa Cruz Mountains family that's been cooking up here for generations. Come for the burger. Stay for the ghost stories.
